## What It Does (For Supply Chain People)

Most planning teams manage hundreds or thousands of SKUs using the same replenishment logic, the same safety stock formula, and the same forecast model. Regardless of how different those products actually behave. A fast-moving stable product and an intermittent spare part have nothing in common, but they often get treated identically. The result is stockouts on critical items, overstock on slow movers, and forecasts that nobody trusts.

**Supply Chain Demand Profiler** gives your team a structured way to understand your portfolio before making planning decisions:

- **What kind of demand does each SKU actually have?** Fast and stable? Slow and lumpy? Completely erratic? The tool classifies every product using ADI/CV methodology into four demand profiles: Smooth, Lumpy, Intermittent, and Erratic - each requiring a different planning approach.
- **Which products actually matter?** ABC/XYZ segmentation identifies your high value stable movers vs. your unpredictable long-tail (so you know where to focus planning effort and where to simplify).
- **Which products cluster together?** K-Means clustering groups SKUs by behavior across multiple dimensions, surfacing patterns that ABC/XYZ alone misses.
- **Is your current forecast method actually appropriate?** The tool compares adaptive forecasting models against demand profiles and flags mismatches - products where the forecast method is structurally wrong for the demand type.
- **Where is inventory risk hiding?** SMOG risk scoring (Slow-moving, Moving, Obsolete, Good) at SKU level, with portfolio rationalization suggestions based on demand history, forecast coverage, and lifecycle signals.

The output is not another dashboard. It is a structured, actionable classification of your entire portfolio, ready for S&OP, PLM, and replenishment decisions.
